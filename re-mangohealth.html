RE: MangoHealth Home Redesign

To provide eye prescription exams to the third-world, Theia is a low-cost, full eye examination kit. All you need is a mobile phone, cardboard, scissors, and a pair of eyes.

Focus

+ Cardboard Housing Kit
+ iOS Application

Upcoming Dosage Button

Problem: In order for a user to take a dose prior to the dosage time, the user must go from home to schedule to the specific dosage cell and then press take dosage. The process to take dosage from home includes 3 screens and 3 clicks.

Focus

+ Concise workflow to take medication
+ Emphasis on medication intake
+ Accessibility with larger text

Dosage Button

The main motivation behind creating the dosage button was to emphasis one of the most valuable functions of the application: recording user's medication intake. For this function to hold value, the user must consistently input their dosage. Therefore, I increased this tasks prominence, making it the only action on the home screen. This exclusivity shifts the home screen from an area of access to an area of action.

Taking Medication

With the previously stated workflow for taking dosage, minimizing screens and user actions was vital, but the action could be as dismissive as simply pressing a button on the home screen. Therefore, to require some more investment and time from the user, the action of taking the prescription is holding the home dosage button. And for their efforts, they are visually rewarded, which also serves as indication, a transition to the next prescription, and design inconvinience.

Focus

+ Visual incentive
+ Communicate result of action
+ Seamless transition
+ Feasability for animation in Android and iOS
